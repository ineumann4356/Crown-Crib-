Crown Cut

Triggered by Royal Decrees 4–6.

After crib discard, reveal 2 non-King cards. The dealer chooses 1 as the Starter Card. Replace any revealed King until 2 non-King choices are available.

Crib Scoring

Events 1–3: crib uses the same Scoring Law.

Events 4–6: crib scores normally.

Official Round Order

  1. Deal 3 private cards to each player
  2. Set the Knight’s Table based on player count
  3. Roll Event Die
  4. Draft from the Knight’s Table
  5. Resolve Royal Decree effects if the Event Die was 4–6
  6. Discard 1 card to the crib
  7. Reveal the Starter Card
    • Event 1–3: normal starter cut
    • Event 4–6: Crown Cut
  8. Pegging
  9. Score hands
  10. Dealer scores crib
    • Event 1–3: crib uses the same Scoring Law
    • Event 4–6: crib scores normally
  11. Rotate dealer clockwise

Deal. Table. Roll. Draft. Decree. Discard. Cut. Peg. Score.

Wild Kings Reminder

Kings are wild during hand and crib scoring. A King may count as any rank from Ace through King, whichever creates the best score, but it never changes suit. 5 of a kind scores 20 points normally and 25 points during Perfect Pairs.

During pegging, Kings behave like normal Kings and count as 10. A King used as a Jack may score nobs if its original suit matches the Starter Card.
